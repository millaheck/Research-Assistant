QUALITATIVE X QUANTITATIVE RESEARCH

Qualitative refers to phenomenon that can be observed but not measured while quantitative refers to the ones that can be quantified.

Qualitative research

  • Types: Observations, one-on-one interviews, case study research, focus groups, ethnographic research, phenomenology, and grounded theory.
  • Analyse: Collects non-numerical data such as words, images, and sounds.
  • Objective: seeks to understand a phenomenon within a real-world context through the use of interviews and observation.

Quantitative research

  • Types: Descriptive, Correlational, Causal-Comparative/Quasi-Experimental, and Experimental Research.
  • Analyse: Collects numerical data and analyzes it using statistical methods.
  • Objective: seeks to understand the causal or correlational relationship between variables through testing hypotheses.

METHODOLOGY OPTIONS

Your research methodology discusses and explains the data collection and analysis methods you used in your research. Methodological options refers to the decisions made regarding the research methods and techniques used in a study.

Case study

  • Types: Descriptive, explanatory, multiple case studies, intrisenc, instrumental.
  • How or why kinds of research questions to understand some phenomenon in depth.
  • Objective: Explain, describe or explore events in the everyday contexts in which they occur.

Literature review

  • They give an overview of a topic's theoretical background. It is necessary in any research.
  • It is a survey of scholarly sources on a specific topic.
  • Objective: Provide a foundation of knowledge on a topic.

Questionnaire

  • Types: Structured, Unstructured, Open ended, Close ended, Dichotomous, Multiple choice, Likert scale.
  • Provide structured and standardized responses, making the analysis process more streamlined and less time-consuming.
  • Objective: Collecting information about people's knowledge, beliefs, attitudes, and behaviour.

Field study

  • Involves conducting observations and collecting data in a natural setting.
  • Occurs over time, beginning at the moment a field researcher enters the field and continuing as interactions are happening in the field, as the researcher writes up descriptive notes.
  • Objective: Observe and interpret the subject of study in its natural environment.

RESEARCH PHASES

Consists of a series of systematic procedures that a researcher must go through in order to generate knowledge that will be considered valuable by the project and focus on the relevant topic.

Preparatory phase

  • When you will organize everything that will be needed to do the research, how it will be.

Data collection phase

  • When you will apply the tasks you had defined in the preparatory phase.

Data analysis phase

  • When you will analyse the data colected and reflect on possible cause and effects, context, etc.
  • Analysis of the discoveries made during the research, in the data collection phase.
